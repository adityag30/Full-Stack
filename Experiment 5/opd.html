OUTPATIENT DEPARTMENT(OPD)

OPD stands for Outpatient Department, a section in hospitals or clinics where patients receive consultations, diagnosis, and treatment for minor illnesses or routine check-ups without needing to be admitted for an overnight stay. It serves as the initial, non-emergency point of contact with medical professionals, allowing patients to return home the same day.

Key Aspects of OPD:

Services: Consultations, specialist advice, diagnostic tests (X-rays, blood tests), minor procedures, vaccinations, and follow-ups.
Function: Functions as the first point of care, often leading to referrals to specific departments.
Differences from IPD: Unlike the Inpatient Department (IPD), no hospital admission (over 24 hours) is required.
Access: Appointments can often be booked online through portals like ORS Patient Portal using an Aadhaar number or via teleconsultation platforms.
Insurance: Many health insurance policies offer coverage for OPD consultations and tests.

Standard Consultation Process

  1. Registration: Book an appointment at the physical registration counter or at theonline portal.
  2. Consultation:The doctor reviews your medical history, symptoms, and performs a physical exam.
